Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Course Objectives

An in-depth understanding of HCI principles, methods, and applications.

Course Objectives

1. Introduction to HCI and Its Importance

Provide an understanding of the history and significance of Human-Computer Interaction as a multidisciplinary field, combining principles from computer science, design, psychology, and cognitive sciences.

  • Definition and scope of HCI.
  • The evolution of HCI.
  • The role of HCI in modern computing systems.
  • The interdisciplinary nature of HCI (involving psychology, design, computer science, sociology, and ergonomics).

2. Understanding User Needs and Contexts

Introduce techniques for understanding users, their needs, goals, and the contexts in which they interact with systems.

  • User research techniques such as surveys, interviews, and observations.
  • Personas and user scenarios.
  • Task analysis and user modeling.
  • Contextual inquiry and ethnographic methods.

3. Design Principles and Guidelines

Expose students to key design principles and guidelines for creating effective and user-friendly interfaces.

  • Principles of user interface (UI) design: Consistency, feedback, affordances, visibility, and flexibility.
  • Heuristic evaluation and the use of guidelines (e.g., Nielsen’s heuristics).
  • Universal design and accessibility principles (e.g., WCAG guidelines).
  • Design patterns and UI conventions.

4. Usability and User Experience (UX)

Teach students how to measure usability and ensure a positive user experience through thoughtful design and testing.

  • Usability attributes: Efficiency, effectiveness, satisfaction, and learnability.
  • UX principles: Emotional design, aesthetic-usability effect, and experience goals.
  • Usability testing methods: Formative vs. summative testing, user trials, task performance metrics, think-aloud protocol, etc.
  • Usability metrics and quantitative vs. qualitative methods.

5. Prototyping and Iterative Design

Provide students with the skills needed to develop prototypes, iterate upon them, and refine designs based on feedback.

  • Low-fidelity vs. high-fidelity prototypes (sketches, wireframes, mockups).
  • Tools for creating prototypes (e.g., paper prototypes, Balsamiq, Figma, Adobe XD, InVision).
  • Rapid prototyping and its role in iterative design processes.
  • Design thinking methodology and agile development approaches in HCI.

6. Cognitive and Psychological Aspects of HCI

Introduce the cognitive aspects of human-computer interaction, including how people perceive, think, and act while interacting with systems.

  • Cognitive load theory and its relevance to interface design.
  • Mental models and how they affect user interaction.
  • Perception and attention: How users interpret visual information.
  • Memory and learning: How users recall and recognize information.

7. Interaction Styles and Techniques

Teach students about different types of interaction styles and input techniques that affect how users interact with systems.

  • Command-line interfaces vs. graphical user interfaces.
  • Touch-based interfaces, voice interaction, and gesture-based controls.
  • Tangible user interfaces and augmented reality (AR).
  • Natural language processing (NLP) and multimodal interfaces.

8. Mobile and Web Interface Design

Address the unique challenges involved in designing interfaces for mobile and web applications.

  • Designing for different screen sizes, orientations, and resolutions (responsive design).
  • Mobile-specific design considerations: Touch interactions, gestures, notifications, battery life, etc.
  • Web accessibility and mobile-first design principles.
  • Designing adaptive interfaces for various platforms (Android, iOS, web).

9. Ethical Considerations in HCI

Explore the ethical implications of designing interactive systems and the potential consequences for users.

  • Privacy and data security issues in user interactions.
  • The impact of user surveillance and tracking technologies.
  • Accessibility and inclusivity in system design.
  • Designing for social good: Ensuring systems are not discriminatory.

10. Emerging Trends and Future Directions in HCI

Explore cutting-edge developments in HCI and their potential impacts on future technologies and user experiences.

  • Virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) in HCI.
  • AI and machine learning’s role in HCI (personalized experiences, predictive interfaces).
  • Internet of Things (IoT) and ambient computing.
  • Conversational user interfaces (chatbots, voice assistants).
